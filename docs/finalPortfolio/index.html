d).

I first used a grouped bar chart to show average genetic share across regions for the three different kits. The biggest story from this graph is how the Ancestry DNA kit differs most greatly from the other two kits, specifically in Central Africa. Ancestry reports higher numbers for NW Europe, and lower numbers for SE Europe and West Africa, compared to the other two kits. This insight points to the idea that Ancestry may categorize regions differentyly, or that Ancestry makes more rigid groupings for their DNA test kits.

Next, I made a scatter plot that has the genetic share of twin a on the x-axis and the genetic share of twin b on the y-axis. This makes it easy for us to distinguish differences among twins. The diagonal line through the middle of the chart represents perfect agreement between the genetic share of twin a and twin b. The main story of this graphic is that there’s very similar genetic shares between twins, regardless of what test they use. The genetic share of each twin pair was very close to the diagonal line, indicating similar genetic share.

b).

How the components of the net promoter score for our business have changed over time?

For our business, the Promoter component has grown significantly from January 2019-February 2020, from 39% to 63%.

The passives share has declined during this time period, from about 51% to 13%.

The demoter share has also slightly increased during this time period, but not significantly. Demoter share went from 10% in January of 2019, to 25% in February of 2020.

Overall, the increase of promoters and decrease of passives, likely contributed to the increasing NPS for Our Business from January 2019 to February of 2020.

c).

The story that I am trying to tell with the data is that Tanzania has made notable progress over the past 25 years in the realm of family planning. Over the years, the rate of contraception use has increased substantially in Tanzania from about 6% to 38%. The fertility rate has also decreased during these years, likely due to the increased rate of contraception. Additionally, Tanzania has made a slight decrease in the percent of women experiencing an unmet need for family planning. While this is progress, the percent of unmet need for family planning has only decreased by 5%, from 27% down to 22%. My story highlights this slower decrease, when compared to the sharp increase in modern contraception. By overlaying the lines in my line chart, you are able to easily see that contraception use is increasing at at much quicker rate, than unmet need for family planning is decreasing. This would be more difficult to make this comparison if I had used side by side line charts, or faceting.

b).

I created a scatterplot that compares movie budget to Rotten Tomato score. I concatenated my graphic to also show average Rotten Tomato score by genre. The trendline demonstrates that from 1970-2010, as movie budget increases, Rotten Tomato score decreases.

When designing my graphic, in addition to encoding X and Y, I choose to encode the color channel with MPAA rating. Color was also used to encode average Rotten Tomato score on the bar chart by genre, using the redyellowgreen scheme to make it easy to compare ratings across genres.

I also choose to layer the scatter plot with a regression line to show trends in Rotten Tomato score. I added in sliders that update each mark automatically as the slider is moved. The sliders make it so that you can filter by specific year ranges, and marks and the trendline will automatically update.

Additionally, I choose to use a logarithmic scale for the x-axis, because it better fit the wide range of budgets that the data set presented.

One of the alternatives that I considered was instead of concatinating and doing an Average Rotten Tomato Score by genre, doing the Rotten Tomato Score and Budget graph with facets for each genre. I decided to not do this though, because when I tested it out, there were so many facets and it was hard to make comparisons between groups.
